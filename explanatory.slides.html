A Brief Analysis on California’s Climate¶

Introduction¶

Climate change is one of the largest environmental threats humanity has ever faced. The burning of fossil fuels and anthropogenic carbon emissions have contributed to a global atmospheric CO2 content of 405 ppm(parts per million) by 2017, around 40% higher than the pre-industrial level of 280ppm back in the mid-1700s . Climate change encompasses global phenomena such as increased temperatures, sea level rise, and extreme weather events such as droughts and monsoons. At this current pace, the extent of climate change effects may cause catastrophic environmental and societal damage as the earth systems struggle to adapt to this unprecedented rate of change. Although climate change is a global phenomenon, its impacts can very heterogenous across different regions. The chosen region for this detailed climate study was California. The first part of this analysis examines global and regional temperature trends using moving averages starting from 1895 to 2015. This is done primarily to visualize historical temperature trends and how California as a region compares to global average temperatures. Graphing historical temperature trends would also help show significant patterns The second part of the study draws relationships between climate parameters using statistical regression. California into temperature anomalies in California and how these may be correlated to atmospheric carbon dioxide levels and Palmer Drought Severity Index (PDSI) values, which use temperature and precipitation data to quantify long-term drought events. PDSI values can range anywhere from -10 (extreme dry conditions) and +10 (extreme wet conditions)

Categorize the PDSI¶

Based on national drought migration center, drought was categorized into 10 categorization. But, in order to facilitate the data ananlysis, we shortened to 7 categorization¶
  • ext_drorepresents the extreme drought was observed(pdsi<=-4.0)
  • droughtrepresents the droght or severe drought was observed(pdsi from -3.99 to -2.0)
  • mil_drorepresents the mild or slight droght events were observed(pdsi from -1.99 to -0.5)
  • normalrepresents the normal year (pdsi from -0.49 to 0.49)
  • mil_wetrepresents the mild or slight wet events were observed(pdsi from 0.5 to 1.99)
  • wet represents the wet events were observed(pdsi from 2.0 to 3.99)
  • ext_wet represents the extreme wet events were observed(pdsi >= 4.0)

Conclusion¶

Part1¶

  • Overall, the global average temperatures are lower than the CA average temperature where the global temperature ranges from 6-8 degrees Celsius and the US temperature ranges from 14-16(excluding extreme case)
  • Based on both rolling 10 MA graphs and trends graphs, there is a short dip in temperature from 1800 to 1830
  • Both graphs show that the temperatures have been increasing since 1960

Part2¶

Based on above two regressional graphs, we can see that pdsi and temperature anormaly has negative correlation with very small p-value(0.012) and co2_level and temerature anormaly has positive correlation with very small p-value(0.007), which means we have statistically significant evidence to show the relationship between temperature and CO2 level, and the relationship between temperature and PDSI. The linear model with dummy variables shows that only wet events will have significant statistics evidence of the relationship between the events and the temperature anormaly while the relationship between drought events and temperature anormaly is not as statistically significant as the relationship between the wet events and temperature anormaly.

Limitations¶

The timespan of the pdsi data is relatively short No further composite analysis was used due to the time and capability limitation.
